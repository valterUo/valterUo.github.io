Why does it work?

The abstract foundation of quantum computing is based on quantum mechanics.

See section Introduction to quantum mechanics in book Quantum Computation and Quantum Information 10th Anniversary Edition.

Quantum mechanics is one of the most accurate theory in physics. The book represents four simple postulates that can be used to derive all of the consequences. Actually the postulates look a lot like the mathematical formulation for quantum computing. So if you know quantum computing you can actually understand quantum mechanics easier.
Single qubit systems

$|\psi\rangle = a|0\rangle + b|1\rangle$

where $a,b \in \Complex$ are amplitudes and $|a|^2 + |b|^2 = 1$.

The states $|0\rangle = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$ and $|1\rangle = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ are called computational basis and they are the most common choice for computations.

Multiple qubits

\[\begin{aligned} |\psi\rangle &= (a_0|0\rangle + b_0|1\rangle) \otimes (a_1|0\rangle + b_1|1\rangle) \\ & = a_0a_1|00\rangle + a_0b_1|01\rangle + b_0a_1|10\rangle + b_0b_1|11\rangle. \end{aligned} \]

We see that $n$ qubit system has $2^n$ amplitudes (i.e. the coefficients).

This means that with 500 qubit fully connected quantum computer we can express more amplitides than the estimated number of atoms in the universe.

Evolution of quantum systems

Initial system of qubits (usually $|00\ldots0\rangle$)

Apply gates the qubits. Gates are modelled as unitary transformations.

Measure the system. The system ends up to some classical state. The sequence of classical bits is the result of the quantum computation.

Two big advantages of quantum computing

Superposition

One qubit can be in multiple states simultaneously.

For example, the most conventional method to put a qubit to a superposition is to apply Hadamar-gate

$H|0\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{|0\rangle + |1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$.

Entanglement

Entanglement roughly means that two or more qubits are connected and they can interact in ways such that the state of each qubit cannot be described independently of the state of the other qubits.

For example, the most conventional method to entangle two qubits is to use CNOT gate:

CNOT$(|x\rangle \otimes |y\rangle) = |x\rangle \otimes |x + y \mod 2\rangle$.

We see that the first qubit $x$ affect on the value of the second qubit. In the case of CNOT, the first qubit is called a control qubit and the second one is called a target qubit. Again, the CNOT gate has a unitary matrix representation as 4x4 matric. This representation is actually quite simple.

Some problems in quantum information processing

that are not fatal problems

These challenges are especially restricting from data storing perspective.

No-cloning theorem

Theoretically provable theorem that quantum states cannot be copied or cloned.

See Box 12.1: The no-cloning theorem in book Quantum Computation and Quantum Information.

Implies no-broadcasting theorem which says that a quantum state cannot be conveyed to two or more recipients.

Measuring destroys states

Measuring a qubit collapses it to either 0 or 1. After the measurement we cannot modify the measured qubit anymore.

Error

Quantum computing is never performed in absolutely isoleted system and the environment creates decoherence and other quantum noise.

We can tackle the error by developing quantum error correction.

We could not measure an absolutely isolated system.

Quantum machine learning

Just ultimate edge of hype?

What is surprising here is that quantum computing and ML actually fit together naturally. So this is not so much just combining two hyped fields together. I am going into the details how quantum machine learning works. I just show some general outlines and use it as a motivating example to prove why it is important to consider data management from quantum computing perspective.

Data and hardware combinations

Personally, I divide the field into two subfields depending on the used hardware.

  • Restricted Boltzman machines on quantum annealers
  • Kernel methods (such as support vector machines) on variational circuits (based on gate-model)

Why QC and ML are so good match?

Both quantum computing and ML are probabilistic. We can use quantum states to describe statistical properties in data.

Data management perspective: how do we encode classical data into quantum computers?

For example, we take a qubit and a data set. For each feature in the data set, we rotate the qubit based on some predefined rules. This encodes the data but th encoding has proved to be unuseful and does not provide speed-up.

A good encoding for classical data is still an open question.

Naturally a good encoding would boost up quantum machine learning a lot.

For more information, see Machine Learning With Quantum Computers | Tutorial | NeurIPS 2021

It is actually funny that Maria in the video says that quantum computing for big data was initially an advertisement campaing in the sense that you just cannot upload big data into a quantum computer. All the data needs to be encoded into a quantum computer so already this encoding step is challenging. And she mentions that it is difficult to argue if quantum computer can perform some big data application faster. But possibly CS community should not always be focusing on speed of the algorithms. We even forget to analyze space requirments. Besides that we should take into account sustainability aspects i.e. energy consumption of the computers and also other ethical aspects. It is also possible that quantum machine learning is more explainable ML than the conventional ML. So even if some quantum algorithm performs not faster than the classical algorithm it is still worth to study if it has other advantages. This video also describes more encodings.

BMW quantum computing challenge:

Sensor positions optimization

Problem

Cars, especially self-driving cars, require multiple sensors to monitor the car's environment. Positioning the sensors is a combinatorically difficult problem and testing different combinations is slow.

In this challenge BMW wanted to research possibilities of quantum computing in the sensor positioning process.

New Angles from Right Range:

Optimizing Car Sensor Positioning with D-Wave Hybrid Quantum Computers

Valter Uotila & Sardana Ivanova

Sensor positions optimization problem

Position $n$ sensors onto a car's surface so that

  • the environment is covered as well as possible,
  • the sensors do not overlap more than necessary and
  • the total price of the sensors is minimized.

Example of car data

Initializing the binary variables requires sampling points from the car's surface.

Core idea of solution

We utilized Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization model and followed the standard approach in D-wave's examples and in the paper Ising formulations of many NP problems.

Example of sensors

Initializing the binary variables requires sampling points from the environment.

Core idea of solution

  • Formalizing problem
  • Defining suitable binary variables
  • Identifying and creating suitable constraints
  • Implementing variables and constraints with Ocean software framework

Constraints

  • Constraint 1: selecting sufficiently many sensors to cover the environment
  • Constraint 2: optimizing overlap of the sensor views
  • Constraint 3: minimizing the total price

Constraints are translated into objective functions and summed together.

Implementation

Implemented with D-wave's Ocean software and computed on D-wave's hybrid quantum computer.

Implementation draft is available on Github as Jupyter notebook .
